Introduction

Hi, I’m Tova! I graduated with a degree in biomedical engineering, but late into my degree I realized that my true passion was programming. I’ve tried for many years to attain a job in programming, but unfortunately without success. Among the ways that I’ve taught myself are through textbook, course specializations on data structures and OOP on Coursera, doing algorithms on Codewars, and working through hundreds of algorithms in a collection intended for interviews. However, I ran into a problem when I applied for interviews. There were a lot of technical things that I had no knowledge of, and it seemed that there was a broad expanse to programming which I still hadn’t touched. I realized that I was lacking a strong grasp of fundamentals which people with a more formal education have. I need structure, a framework, and I need mentors I can turn to. That’s exactly what Founders & Coders offers. Another factor that is appealing about the course is the people. From what I’ve researched, the students who attend the course are genuinely happy to be there, and the atmosphere is positive and buoyant. I think the people around you have a huge impact on how you learn and work. I really want to be around people who are as passionate and excited about learning as I am. Also, it’s exciting (S) that the course is being run (S) by volunteers, and was founded through idealism, love of the material (S). That’s a factor that I have tremendous admiration for.
